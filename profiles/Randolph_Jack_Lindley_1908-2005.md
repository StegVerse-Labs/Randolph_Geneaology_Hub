# Col. Jack Lindley Randolph (1908/1909–2005)

## Summary

**Colonel Jack Lindley Randolph** was a U.S. Army Air Corps / U.S. Air Force
officer born in **Warrenton, Clatsop County, Oregon**, who became the commanding
officer of the **449th Bomb Group (H)** during World War II. He flew and led
combat missions across Europe, including the bombing of Monte Cassino and Ploiești,
and later continued as a senior officer into the early USAF era.

He married **Nellie Althea Cash** in New Mexico after WWII and later settled in
**Ventura, California**, where the couple became original owners of the family
home that remained in the Randolph line. Jack was known as a brilliant aviator,
leader, and storyteller, remembered for his integrity, courage, and proud military
legacy.

## Military Service & War Record

Jack entered early military aviation through **Randolph Field** (San Antonio, TX), as part of one of the earliest graduating flying classes (~1931).  
He later commanded the **449th Bombardment Group (H)** based at **Grottaglie, Italy**, where he led missions across the European theater.

### WWII Operations

- Monte Cassino Campaign  
- Ploiești Oil Fields (Romania)  
- Air Offensive Europe  
- Southern France  
- Rome–Arno  
- Balkans  
- Germany Campaigns  

### Decorations

- **Silver Star** (with Oak Leaf Clusters)  
- **Distinguished Flying Cross** (with Oak Leaf Clusters)  
- **Air Medal** (with 3 Oak Leaf Clusters)  
- European–African–Middle Eastern Campaign Medal  
  - Bronze Service Stars for:
    - Rome–Arno  
    - Southern France  
    - Normandy  
    - Air Offensive Europe  
    - Air Combat Balkans  
    - Germany  

> His combat leadership record is considered historically significant within the 449th.

## The Flying Horsemen — 40th Reunion Address (1983)

Jack authored a reunion welcome speech for the 449th Bomb Group, recalling harsh conditions at Grottaglie and honoring the resilience of the men he commanded.

> “So again, welcome Flying Horsemen — to our 1st Reunion — our 40th anniversary.
> I am extremely proud to have been a part of this outstanding group and to share
> in its cherished traditions and memories.”  
> — *Col. Jack Randolph, USAF (Ret.)*

He was a man who carried war memories heavily, but wore honor lightly.
